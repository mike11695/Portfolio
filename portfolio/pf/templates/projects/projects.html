List.it

Website

List.it is a web application that I developed as my senior project in 2020 at SUNY Potsdam. The idea for the site came about after seeing my mom and aunt using Facebook Marketplace and groups to sell and auction off items that they owned. I felt that it was a quite bit difficult to use for them as they had to track bids and offers throughout different groups, which can quickly become confusing. I wanted to see if I could make an application that would be more efficient in helping users manage their items and know who to contact regarding items.
When developing the site, I also had a bit of inspiration from other web sites such as eBay, LetGo and Carousell. Users are able to upload pictures of their objects to the site and then create items using the photos. Using these created items, they can either add them to listings to trade or auction or use them to offer on other listings. Users are also allowed to create wishlists and wishlist listings to find certain items that may not currently be listed on site. Users will only be able to see listings in about a 30 mile radius of where they live, but they can use a search function to increase the search radius up to 50 miles.
List.it includes some additional features such as allowing users to communicate through the site, leave ratings, host events, make payments on site using PayPal and more. To read more about these features, I have included the report I had made for the project below.

PetTP

Website

PetTP was a project that I developed as practice for Ruby-on-Rails for users on the site Neopets to list pets that they wanted to exchange for other pets. Originally, a friend had previously owned the site but it was no longer working or being managed, so I offered to recreate it for them.
The site allows a user to add a pet to the site by entering their name and stats using a form. After submitting the form, a call was made to Neopet's API in order to get the pet's color, species and other details using the pet's name. However, Neopets began blocking calls to their API, so I had to change it so that users have to select the pet's color and species themselves. Users are able to edit their pet listing at any time or delete it if they no longer own the pet or no longer want to trade it.
Users are given the option to communicate on site or add a link to their inbox on Neopets for people to contact them. They can easily search for pets they want by using the home page search functions and can view user profiles to see all of the pets they have for trade. Users can also simply look for new homes for their pets instead of trading them by adopting them out if they choose to do so.

StepApp

StepApp was a project that worked on with a team of other students my senior year at SUNY Potsdam. We were tasked with creating an Android/iOS app for an organization at the college that would allow advisors and school students to communicate with one another to prepare for college. Students were also provided with resources on the app that they could use to help prepare themselves.
For my role in the project, I started with developing the chat feature of the app. Using Android Studios and Google Firebase, I was able to develop a layout for the chat feature, and had just begun implementing the messaging feature. However, the project was cancelled after about two months as the client could not afford the app and did not want us to develop the app with no compensation.
Although I was not able to complete much as I would have liked, what I did learn inspired me into continuing app development one day. Android Studios was very convenient and easy to pick up, and Firebase provided great support for the app. I also enjoyed working with my team for the short time we had and discussing what we had accomplished and what needed to get done.

Autograder

Autograder was the project I worked on with a team of other students when I took Software Engineering at SUNY Potsdam. The project helped me learned how to use Django/Python, Docker, what sprints are and to participate in them, test driven development and agile methodology. The project was to create a web application for professors and students where the students can submit their coding assignments and the professors can validate the correctness of their code against tests cases.
For the project, I developed some of the models but made most of the views and forms for the other models. I also wrote documentation regarding the forms informing the user how they should be used. Besides the view and forms, I help make the site visually pleasing using HTML/CSS/JavaScript and created an invitation system in which professors could enter a number of student emails and invite them to one or more courses. If the student did not have an account on site with the given email, an account would be made for them including a temporary password. An invitation link would then be emailed to students allowing them to join the course, or they can see and accept the invites onsite instead.
